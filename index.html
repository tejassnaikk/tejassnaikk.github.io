About

How I think about the work.

I started in electrical engineering, which is still how I think about data work — as systems with inputs, failure modes, and signals you measure rather than assume. That mindset is why my projects tend to be fully deployed with observability around them, not notebooks that work once.

I like to understand why a design holds before writing the how. When something doesn't fit — Spark blowing past memory on a small instance, a detector under-sizing offshore — I'd rather state the trade-off plainly than paper over it. Defensible and honest beats impressive and brittle.

Right now I'm finishing my MS while building portfolio systems end-to-end, and looking for a data or ML engineering role in the Denver area where shipping reliable systems is the point.
