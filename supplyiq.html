01

The problem

Supply-chain teams need clean analytics and forward-looking demand forecasts, but raw transactional data is messy, multi-grain, and hard to trust. The project builds a layered analytics pipeline that ends in demand forecasts a planner could actually use — with the transformations structured so bad upstream data can't silently corrupt the numbers downstream.

03

Key decisions & tradeoffs

Where the layering and tool choices earn their keep.

Architecture

Medallion (Bronze / Silver / Gold)

Separating raw, cleaned, and business-ready layers makes every transformation debuggable and reprocessable. If upstream data is wrong, you fix Silver and rebuild Gold — bad data never silently reaches the forecasts or the dashboard.

Platform

Databricks for compute, Snowflake for serving

Build the lakehouse on free serverless Databricks, then push curated marts to Snowflake where BI queries are fast and cheap. Right tool per layer instead of forcing one platform to do both, and free-tier throughout.

Forecasting

Prophet and XGBoost, not one or the other

Prophet captures trend and seasonality cleanly and handles holidays; XGBoost captures nonlinear effects and exogenous drivers Prophet can't. Using both covers each other's blind spots.

Editor note. Say how they combine — an ensemble, or Prophet for some series and XGBoost for others — and which performed better.
Serving

Power BI Service for stakeholders

Publishing to a hosted Power BI dashboard makes the forecasts consumable by non-technical planners — the same delivery pattern I used for 100+ stakeholders at LTIMindtree.
